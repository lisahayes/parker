Do You Have a Slab Leak? Parker Slab Leak Risk Checker

Answer a few questions about what you are noticing. We score the symptoms and tell you whether a professional check is warranted.

Slab leaks form under your foundation, which means they rarely show themselves directly. Instead they appear as secondary signs: a floor that feels warmer than it should, a water bill that climbs with nothing to explain it, or the sound of water running when every fixture is off.

This checker scores the symptoms you check against the weighted indicators used in professional assessments. It is a guide, not a diagnosis. A high score means professional electronic or acoustic detection is warranted. Detection is non-invasive and pinpoints the leak before any concrete is opened.

Why slab leaks are common in Parker, CO

Two Parker-specific factors drive slab leak frequency. First, the region's hard water at 9.2 grains per gallon corrodes copper supply lines internally over decades. Homes built in the 1990s and early 2000s in Stonegate, Stroh Ranch, and nearby master-planned communities have copper now entering the mid-pinhole window. Second, bentonite expansive clay in eastern Parker neighborhoods physically moves foundations through the swell-shrink cycle, stressing the rigid plumbing beneath the slab independently of corrosion.

When professional detection is warranted, it uses acoustic listening, thermal imaging, and pinpoint methods to locate the leak before any concrete is opened. That precision is what keeps a slab leak repair manageable. You can also use our hidden water leak detector to confirm whether water is actively leaving your system.
